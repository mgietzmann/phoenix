The purpose of these rituals is to first develop the `Outline.md` and then turn that outline into an `Introduction.md`.

# At the Start

This is where we start - seeding the outline.

## Seed Forming

A lot of ideas start as just a cognitive whisper, a hunch. In this ritual that hunch is developed into an idea that could be related and feels potent and exiciting. The ultimate outcome of this ritual is a "seed" - a short transcript of the idea. However it may take a couple tries before something good enough to transcribe is found.

### Tools

- A pair of boots for walking
- An audio recording device
- Descript (for turning the transcript into text)

### Environment

This is best done walking in a private place where you can think and talk openly. The point here is to go for walks and to think deeply in order to find the idea that was indicated by the hunch.

### Switching

Don't take more than an hour or so at a time with this ritual. 

## Recycling

Here you'll be working back and forth with Claude to figure out what can be reused from material you've written in the past. 

### Tools

- VsCode and Claude
  
### Environment 

This is best done where you have space to think (private can think out loud if need be), is cozy (you'll be working from the machine), and has a good connection (so you can work with Claude).

### Switching 

Step away after an hour or so - if we get too lost in the weeds we'll miss the forest for the trees. 

## Drafting the Funnel

Here you'll be taking what's in your introduction's seed and developing the outline of your funnel. This is a expansion task so it is best done on a walk or at the very least while being up and moving about. The outcome you're looking for here is the points in your funnel, in order, jotted down as notes. You'll then be transferring those notes to `Funnel.md` in your project. 

### Tools

- A pair of boots for walking
- Something to take notes on as you walk. 

### Environment

This is best done walking in a private place where you can think and talk openly. The point here is to go for walks and to think deeply in order to really dig into how you want this funnel to look.

### Switching

Don't take more than an hour or so at a time with this ritual. 

## Treasure Hunting

Time to find treasures (whoppers) that'll support a topic sentence! 

1. Start by creating a list of questions whose answers you think will really power your topic sentence (for example if the topic sentence is "The pacific ocean is a vast environment" think of questions like "how vast" "how to define vastness" "how does it compare to other oceans" etc.) Remember the purpose here is to paint a vivid picture. 
2. Once you're happy with your question list, pull up google search or google scholar's lab AI and start asking away! As you find resources plug them into both zotero and notebooklm and use notebooklm to ask questions and review answers.
3. As you start finding answers plug them into your `Outline.md` and make sure you include a citation!
4. Also as new questions come to mind, add them to your questions list.

Once you feel you've gotten enough material you're done treasure hunting for this topic sentence. 

Also remember, the point here is not to find nice polished sentences - it is just to get fascinating details onto the outline!

### Tools

- Google
- Google Scholar Lab
- NotebookLM
- Zotero
- VsCode

### Environment 

This is best done where you have space to think (private can think out loud if need be), is cozy (you'll be working from the machine), and has a good connection (so you can work with Google, Google Scholar, NotebookLM, and Zotero).

### Switching 

Step away after an hour or so - if we get too lost in the weeds we'll miss the forest for the trees. 

## Take a Step Back

Okay you've got a better sense of the facts now. And you're probably realizing the organization you thought made sense doesn't anymore. So it's time to revisit your funnel and your outline! This is another brainstorming activity where the end goal is to jot down some takeaways on reorganization, new questions etc. that you can then work through with Claude. 

### Tools

- Your outline
- Your funnel
- A place to take notes

### Environment

This is best done in a private place where you can think and talk openly. The point here is to really mull over what you have so far and think about how to organize it better, add new things, really make it sing.

### Switching

Don't take more than like 30 minutes on this.
