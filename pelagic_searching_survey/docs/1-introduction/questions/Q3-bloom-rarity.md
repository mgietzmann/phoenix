# Question: How heterogeneous and ephemeral are phytoplankton bloom events?

## Current Question
> When phytoplankton blooms occur, how heterogeneous is their spatial distribution, and how long do productive peaks persist?

---

## Orient

**Audience**
Marine biologists and ecologists; readers may not have specialist knowledge of pelagic oceanography and will need the picture painted.

**Purpose**
To close the patchiness argument against a specific objection: a reader could accept Q2's taxonomy of bloom-driving processes and conclude that blooms are widespread. The counter is that individual bloom events are themselves bounded in space and short-lived in time — a predator encounters not a broad productive region but a moving, dissolving patch. This completes the needle-in-a-haystack setup at the level of individual events, not global statistics.

---

## Sharpen

**Specificity**
Focused on individual bloom events rather than global statistics — specific enough to produce concrete numbers without being limited to a single bloom type or ocean region.

**Falsifiability**
A satisfying answer provides quantified measures of spatial heterogeneity within bloom events (e.g. chlorophyll concentration variance, coefficient of variation across a bloom) and durations of productive peaks (days to weeks), ideally across multiple bloom types (spring blooms, eddy-induced, upwelling-driven). Satellite-based observational studies are the primary source. The question is answerable and the numbers are findable.

**Essentiality**
Essential and direct — this is the core of the patchiness argument at the event level. A predator doesn't experience global bloom statistics; it encounters individual patches that form, drift, and dissolve. The question hits this exactly.

**Causal depth**
Descriptive — the goal is concrete numbers (extent, duration) that make the patchiness tangible. No causal mechanism needs establishing here; the processes were covered in Q2. This question just needs to land the empirical reality of what those processes produce.

---

## Situate

**Sufficiency**
Sufficient on its own. Spatial extent and duration of individual bloom events captures the patchiness argument at the level that matters — what a predator actually encounters. No further follow-ups needed to close this part of the argument.

**Related questions**

**Sequencing**
