# Round 1

You know maybe I'm framing this wrong. I'm trying to get at this needle in a haystack idea but that's probably not even a necessarily notion in and of itself. 

The processes that create primary productivity deny any kind of general abstraction. Some of these processes could be very long lived and lead to maintained productivity. Others will be very very ephemeral. In some places mixing is what matters, in others it is transport of nutrients off of the coast into the marine environment. 

I think the main point here is to paint a picture of just how variegated the environment is, how animals have to be able to deal with seasonal changes, changes of the wind as it were, differences due to the amount of loading from rivers and the like, when stratification, and so on. It is not about a needle in a haystack it is about adaptability. 

That's how this needs to be reframed.

Also the "drifters" paragraph needs to be broken into three paragraphs:
1. What phytoplankton need — nutrients and light (the two requirements)
2. The processes that can set those conditions up — upwelling, fronts, eddies, bathymetry, rivers, dust
3. The dynamic nature of these processes — seasonal, geographic, scale-dependent variability — making the productive environment heterogeneous and ever-changing
