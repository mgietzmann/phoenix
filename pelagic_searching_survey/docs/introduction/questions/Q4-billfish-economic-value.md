# Question: Economic value of billfishes

## Current Question
> What economic value do billfishes provide across sport fishing tourism, artisanal fishing, subsistence fishing, and commercial fishing — and which nations depend on that value, particularly low-income and small island states?

---

## Orient

**Audience**
Marine biologists. They accept that biodiversity loss matters, but may be indifferent or dismissive toward billfishes specifically. They will know the species by name once introduced.

**Purpose**
Establish that billfishes provide concrete economic value to human communities — across sport fishing tourism, artisanal fishing livelihoods, subsistence food sources, and commercial fishing — so that their decline is not just an ecological loss but a human one. This motivates the paper's conservation relevance.

---

## Sharpen

**Specificity**
The question names four specific economic channels (sport fishing tourism, artisanal fishing, subsistence fishing, commercial fishing) and anchors the "who" in national dependence and development level — with emphasis on low-income and small island states where billfish value is most critical. Specific enough to guide a search; broad enough to generalize across regions.

**Falsifiability**
A satisfying answer is a curated set of concrete, citable figures — revenue estimates, livelihoods supported, communities dependent — drawn from the most compelling examples across the four channels. Not exhaustive; sufficient to make the value undeniable to a skeptical reader.

**Essentiality**
Yes. The value question establishes why billfishes matter to people. The conservation argument — that we can only protect what we understand — is made separately and depends on this foundation. The two don't need to be fused into one question.

**Causal depth**
Descriptive only. The goal is to paint a picture of existing value — numbers, communities, livelihoods — not to explain why or how that value arose. No mechanism needs to be established.

---

## Situate

**Sufficiency**
Answering this gets you where you need to go for the introduction. The natural follow-up — what is the threat to this value — is handled elsewhere in the argument and doesn't need to be absorbed here.

**Related questions**
- If compelling figures are hard to find by channel, fall back to: who specifically depends on billfishes (communities, nations, fisheries)?

**Sequencing**
Sits after the ecological introduction to billfishes (what they are, where they live) and before the conservation argument (why understanding their behaviour matters). Requires no prerequisites beyond the reader knowing the species.
